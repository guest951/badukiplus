4. Playing on the console

4.1 Command-line arguments

Badukiplus supports the following command-line arguments:

  • -s n
    Board size n ; usual board sizes are 9 (minimum), 13 or 19 (maximum).
  • -handicap m
    Specifies a handicap of m stones for Black. Overridden by -even option.
  • -komi p
    Specifies a komi of p for White; note that p is a float.
  • -white
    badukiplus plays white.
  • -gtp
    Enters GTP mode (ignores other command-line arguments).
  • -h or --help
    Displays a short help file and exits.
  • -v or --version
    Shows program name and version and exits.

4.2 Console Output

A 9 x 9 board with a few stones will be displayed as:

        a b c d e f g h j
      1 . . . . . . . . . 1
      2 . . . . . O O . . 2
      3 . . O . . . # . . 3
      4 . . O . . . # . . 4
      5 O O ^ O . . . . . 5
      6 . # O # . # # . . 6
      7 . # # # . . . . . 7
      8 . . . . . . . . . 8
      9 . . . . . . . . . 9
        a b c d e f g h j

where '^' is a ko, '#' is black, 'O' is white.

  • Badukiplus prompts for the next move and responds instantaneously.
  • Badukiplus expects moves to be typed as LetterNumber (no spaces), e.g. a1 means a stone in the upper left, j9 is bottom right.
  • Typing "." (without the double quotes) means an immediate resignation and Badukiplus will exit after calculating the final score.
  • Typing "pass" will do exactly that. Badukiplus will continue playing or pass, too. If both players pass Badukiplus will calculate the final score and exit.

4.3 Strength

  • Badukiplus probably ranks around 25 kyu. The algorithm is not very sophisticated, even though its heuristics (set of rules that the engine follows to decide on a move) are more complex than those found in Wallyplus. The original baduki.c was designed as a lightweight, fast Go playing engine.
  • Badukiplus is a weak player, but it knows how to play legal moves. This is achieved in around 90k of code. By comparison, the implementation of GTP 1.0 in Gnu Go is already 75k long, and it is considered incomplete!
  • The main purpose of this program is to serve as a test-bed for a C++ implementation of the Go Text Protocol (GTP). More information on the GTP can be found on the Gnu Go homepage.
